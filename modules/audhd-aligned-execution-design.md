# AuDHD-Aligned Execution Design

---

## Purpose

Enable consistent execution without cognitive overload by eliminating unnecessary decisions and separating thinking from action.

---

## Core Principle

Execution must require zero interpretation.

Thinking and doing are separate phases.

---

## Design Constraints

• Single-path execution (no branching during action)  
• Zero-decision steps once execution begins  
• Pre-defined structure before action  
• No mixing of analysis and execution  

---

## Execution Model

1. Define the task (thinking phase)  
2. Lock the structure (no changes mid-execution)  
3. Execute step-by-step without deviation  

---

## Anti-Patterns (Disallowed)

• Choosing between options during execution  
• Adjusting structure mid-task  
• Mixing planning and doing  
• Multi-path workflows  

---

## Support Mechanisms

• Copy-ready outputs  
• One-step-at-a-time sequencing  
• Clear stopping points  
• Explicit next actions  

---

## Use Case

Applied when:

• performing multi-step workflows  
• interacting with tools (GitHub, Sheets, etc.)  
• executing clinical or operational processes  
• maintaining momentum in creative work  

---

## Result

• Reduced cognitive load  
• Increased consistency  
• Sustained momentum  
• Lower task-switching cost
